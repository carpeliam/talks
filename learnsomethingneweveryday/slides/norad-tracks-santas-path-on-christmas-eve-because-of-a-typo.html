NORAD Tracks Santa's Path on Christmas Eve Because of a Typo

#learnsomethingneweveryday — December 17, 2013
In 1955, Sears placed ads asking kids to call various local phone numbers to reach Santa. The local Colorado Springs number was a typo and actually reached the Continental Air Defense Command (CONAD), predecessor to NORAD. When a little girl called, a colonel answered, and was mad at first, but then played along and asked her what she wanted for Christmas. The pretending evolved into charting Santa's progress, and a tradition was born.

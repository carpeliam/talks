Barns Are Painted Red Because of the Physics of Dying Stars

#learnsomethingneweveryday — May 13, 2013

Barns are painted red because red paint is cheap. Red paint is cheap because the ingredient in red paint, red ochre (2 irons, 3 oxygens), is plentiful. When a star starts to die, it starts to shrink, and as it shrinks, you get a lot of pressure, which leads to an increase in heat, and these fusion reactions lead to the star emitting heavier and heavier elements. The heaviest element it can emit? Iron.
